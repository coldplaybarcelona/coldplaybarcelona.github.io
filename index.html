Coldplay School Research Project

An interdisciplinary exploration of Coldplay's music, career, and impact.

Project Overview

This multidisciplinary research project involves students from different age groups and specialties exploring various aspects of Coldplay's music, career, and impact through creative and technological projects.

Participating Groups:

  • Projects Group (Ages 11-13)
  • Digitalization Group (Ages 15-16)
  • Coding and Robotics Group (Ages 16-17)
  • Technology and Engineering 2 Group (Ages 17-18)

Supervised by: Prof. Francesc Pérez (Pompeu Fabra High School, Martorell, Barcelona, fperez@iespompeufabra.cat).

In this research project focusing on technology and science, students have proposed a wide variety of innovative ideas to make concerts more sustainable. Their proposals range from smart clothing, augmented and virtual reality, smart bands, brain-computer interfaces, and energy-generating smart caps to 3D designs of various concert gadgets, accompanied by the necessary code to make them function. Additionally, they have developed numerous audio visualizers for Coldplay songs, featuring images and icons related to the band that move in response to the volume and change in shape and color according to the pitch—high or low—of the sound frequencies.

To support the students' work, the professor has developed tools that streamline their workflow. These tools include an editor where students can draw and generate SVG or p5.js code, which is then utilized in their Coldplay audio visualizers. The combination of these technologies demonstrates the integration of creative and technical skills in developing sustainable and interactive concert experiences.

On this website, you will find short and visually engaging Pecha-Kucha-style videos created by all the students, as well as the tools developed by the professor. Most importantly, the site showcases the resulting projects from the collective work of all the students.
