Musical Arts Research (work in progress)

An interdisciplinary exploration of Coldplay's music, technology, and creativity

Project Overview

This multidisciplinary research project involves students from different age groups and specialties exploring various aspects of Coldplay's music, career, and impact through creative and technological projects.

Participating groups

  • Project group (Ages 11-13)
  • Digitalization Group (Ages 15-16)
  • Coding Group (Ages 16-17)
  • Robotics Group (Ages 16-17)
  • Technology and Engineering 2 Group (Ages 17-18)

Project Highlights

Students have put forward a range of innovative ideas aimed at enhancing sustainability in concerts. Their proposals include smart clothing, augmented reality experiences, and energy-generating devices. Additionally, they have created audio visualizers for Coldplay songs, incorporating interactive elements that engage the audience.

One of their standout developments is a series of innovative smart bands that change color in response to music, making them accessible and enjoyable for everyone. These wearables leverage the capabilities of the ESP32C3 microcontroller, RGB LED strips, and built-in microphones to deliver a captivating visual experience. Furthermore, the designs incorporate Lilypad components, sensors, and actuators, ensuring that these bands provide an engaging experience for all users, including those in the deaf community.
